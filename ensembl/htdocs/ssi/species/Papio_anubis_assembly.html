The second assembly of the olive baboon (Papio anubis) genome, PapAnu2.0, was provided by the Baylor College of Medicine in June 2012. The assembly has 20 autosomal chromosomes numbered 1-20 and a sex chromosome labelled X. The assembly contains a total sequence length of 2.95Gb with 55.1Mb of gaps. There are 72,500 scaffolds (63,229 unplaced) comprised of 198,931 contigs with a scaffold N50 of 528.9Kb and a contig N50 of 40.2Kb. The N50 size is the length such that 50% of the assembled genome lies in blocks of the N50 size or longer.
