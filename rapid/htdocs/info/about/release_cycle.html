The Ensembl Rapid Release Cycle

With the huge increase in sequencing planned by the Vertebrates Genome Project and Darwin Tree of Life, we have taken the decision to release these new genomes as quickly as possible – ideally within a couple of weeks of receiving them. However the normal Ensembl release cycle is based around a process of data integration that takes several months to complete, from our first receiving an assembly to the public release of the data on our website and FTP site.

This new Rapid Release site therefore takes an approach of minimal integration. We run the standard genebuild pipeline to produce predicted gene models, but at the moment they are not assigned gene symbols, as this requires projection from a reference species such as human.

As a result of this approach, there is currently no variation, regulation or comparative data for these species. In a few cases, such as new assemblies of major species already present on our main site (www.ensembl.org), we may release the data here with additional annotation. We are also working on a reduced compara pipeline that will allow us to assign gene symbols to new genebuilds.
